# TABELA MAINTENANCE WORKFLOW

## DAILY WORKFLOW

Run TABELA daily.

Validate:

* ETF rankings logical
* Leading themes logical
* Weakening themes logical
* Long candidates logical
* Short candidates logical
* Rotation Delta logical
* Stock history file created correctly
* Snapshot history file created correctly

Questions to ask daily:

* Are leading themes persistent?
* Are shorts clustered logically?
* Is market leadership broadening?
* Are institutions rotating internally?

No coding during normal daily workflow.

---

## WEEKLY REVIEW

Review 5 trading sessions.

Analyze:

* Repeated leading themes
* Repeated lagging themes
* Theme persistence
* Breadth changes
* Long consistency
* Short consistency

Questions:

* Which themes attracted institutions repeatedly?
* Which themes lost sponsorship repeatedly?

---

## SHORT ENGINE REVIEW PROCESS

Review separately from long side.

Questions:

* Are shorts concentrated in weakening themes?
* Are shorts repeatedly appearing?
* Are strong companies entering short list?
* Are shorts indicating active distribution?

Remember:

Weak stock does NOT automatically mean short candidate.

Focus:

Institutional distribution behavior.

---

## LONG ENGINE REVIEW PROCESS

Questions:

* Are longs concentrated in strongest themes?
* Are leaders persistent?
* Are leaders supported by strong breadth?

Focus:

Institutional accumulation behavior.

---

## MONTHLY REVIEW

Analyze:

* Theme half life
* Rotation speed
* Sector persistence
* Historical accumulation behavior
* Historical distribution behavior

---

## ARCHITECTURE DISCIPLINE

Do NOT change architecture frequently.

Observe first.

Minimum observation period:

10 live sessions.

Do not redesign protected modules without strong evidence.

Protected modules:

* RS engine
* Composite engine
* Long engine
* Snapshot engine

Allowed frequent changes:

* Theme mapping
* ETF quality filtering
* Classification improvements

Architecture principle:

Stable systems improve through observation, not constant redesign.
